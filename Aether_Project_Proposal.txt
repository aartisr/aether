1. Proposal Summary
I am proposing to design and build Aether, a student-centered digital platform that helps students manage stress, build emotional resilience, and connect with peer support in a safe and responsible way. I chose this project because mental health challenges are one of the most urgent problems students face today, yet many students still avoid getting help due to stigma, cost, or fear of being judged.

Aether addresses that gap by combining three core ideas: private reflection tools, guided resilience-building pathways, and optional peer support matching. My goal is to create a practical, ethical, and technically strong solution that supports student well-being while respecting privacy and student autonomy.

2. Problem Statement
In school communities, students regularly face pressure from academics, family expectations, social dynamics, and uncertainty about their future. Many students need support before reaching a crisis point, but current options are often reactive rather than preventive.

I believe students need a tool that is:
- Easy to access and use daily.
- Non-judgmental and student-friendly.
- Privacy-conscious by default.
- Focused on early support, not only emergency response.

Without this type of support, stress can quietly escalate and negatively impact attendance, performance, confidence, and long-term well-being.

3. Proposed Solution
With Aether, I will build a platform that allows students to:
- Reflect safely through journaling and mood check-ins.
- Understand patterns in their emotional well-being through simple analytics and summaries.
- Follow structured resilience plans with actionable, small weekly habits.
- Access peer support pathways where appropriate, with clear safety boundaries.

Aether is not meant to replace counselors, teachers, or guardians. It is meant to complement existing support systems by helping students recognize challenges earlier and take healthier steps sooner.

4. Why This Project Matters
This project is important for both educational and social reasons:
- It directly supports student success by improving emotional readiness to learn.
- It promotes preventive mental health support, not just crisis intervention.
- It demonstrates ethical technology design, especially around privacy and fairness.
- It creates a scalable model that could be adapted by schools and colleges.

As a student, I am motivated by the belief that technology should not only be smart, but also humane. Aether reflects that belief.

5. Implementation Plan
I will implement Aether in phased milestones to ensure quality and realism.

Phase 1: Research and Requirements (Week 1-2)
I will review trusted mental health and education resources, define user stories, and document functional and ethical requirements.

Phase 2: Core Platform Build (Week 3-6)
I will build the core application with journaling, check-ins, resilience pathway modules, and foundational backend services.

Phase 3: Safety, Privacy, and Testing (Week 7-8)
I will add safeguards, validate privacy settings, test major workflows, and perform usability checks.

Phase 4: Evaluation and Final Delivery (Week 9-10)
I will evaluate outcomes using predefined success metrics, document lessons learned, and present a final demonstration.

6. Success Metrics
To evaluate whether Aether is effective, I will measure:
- Completion rate of weekly check-ins.
- User retention over multiple weeks.
- Improvement in self-reported resilience habits.
- Usability feedback from student testers.
- Stability and reliability of the application during tests.

These metrics will help me assess both technical performance and meaningful student impact.

7. Ethics and Safety Commitment
Because this project touches student well-being, I will prioritize responsible design:
- No diagnostic claims and no replacement of licensed mental health care.
- Clear crisis escalation guidance and resource links.
- Privacy-first data handling and minimal data collection.
- Fairness-focused testing to reduce bias in support recommendations.
- Transparent communication about what the system can and cannot do.

I want this project to model the type of ethical responsibility that educational technology should meet.
