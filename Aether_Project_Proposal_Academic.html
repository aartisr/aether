1. Introduction and Purpose

I am proposing to design and build Aether, a privacy-first digital platform that helps students manage stress, strengthen resilience, and connect with safe peer-support pathways. I selected this project because student mental health is one of the most urgent challenges in education today, yet many students still hesitate to seek support due to stigma, fear of judgment, or limited access to early intervention resources. Through this project, I want to create a practical and ethical technology solution that supports students before stress escalates into crisis.

2. Problem Statement

Students frequently experience pressure from academics, social expectations, family responsibilities, and uncertainty about the future. Existing support systems are essential, but they are often engaged only after problems become severe. I believe students need accessible tools that encourage proactive, everyday emotional support. A successful solution should be easy to use, non-judgmental, privacy-respecting, and grounded in responsible design.

3. Proposed Solution: Aether

Aether will provide a structured and student-friendly ecosystem with four core capabilities:

  • Private journaling and regular emotional check-ins to support reflection.
  • Simple trend insights to help students recognize patterns in well-being.
  • Guided resilience pathways with realistic, habit-based actions.
  • Peer-support navigation with clear safety boundaries and escalation guidance.

Aether is intended to complement, not replace, counselors, teachers, and families. Its purpose is to improve early awareness, promote healthy coping behaviors, and make support more approachable for students.

4. Academic and Social Value

This project is academically valuable because it integrates software engineering, user-centered design, ethics, and impact evaluation into one coherent product. It is socially valuable because it directly addresses a high-priority issue affecting student success, attendance, and long-term well-being. I also believe this project can become a scalable model that schools can adapt to different communities and support systems.

5. Implementation Plan and Timeline

  • Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Research, requirements, and success criteria.
  • Phase 2 (Weeks 3-6): Build core platform modules and workflows.
  • Phase 3 (Weeks 7-8): Safety, privacy, testing, and usability refinement.
  • Phase 4 (Weeks 9-10): Evaluation, documentation, and final presentation.

6. Evaluation and Expected Outcomes

I will evaluate project effectiveness using measurable outcomes such as check-in completion rates, multi-week user retention, resilience habit adoption, usability feedback, and overall system reliability. These metrics will help me assess both technical quality and real student benefit.

7. Ethics, Privacy, and Safety Commitment

Since this project concerns student well-being, I will prioritize ethical guardrails throughout development. Aether will avoid diagnostic claims, include clear crisis-resource signposting, use privacy-first data handling, and incorporate fairness-aware review practices for recommendation behavior. I am committed to building a tool that is not only functional, but also trustworthy and responsible.
